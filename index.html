Instructions: Gmail (Browser)

  1. Click on "Copy Signature to Clipboard".
  2. Open Google Mail and go to Settings (Gear icon > "See all settings").
  3. In the "General" tab, scroll down to the "Signature" section.
  4. Click on "Create new", give your signature a name (e.g., "Planet"), and click "Create".
  5. Paste the copied signature into the text box on the right (using Ctrl+V or Cmd+V).
  6. Below, set the Signature defaults for new emails and replies/forwards to your new signature.
  7. Scroll to the bottom and click "Save Changes".

On a mobile browser (e.g., Chrome/Safari on your phone): You must first request the "Desktop site" in your browser's settings. The page will reload, and you can then follow the exact same steps above.

Instructions: Outlook (Desktop App)

  1. Click on "Copy Signature to Clipboard".
  2. Open Outlook and create a New Email.
  3. In the ribbon, click "Signature" > "Signatures...".
  4. Click "New", enter a name (e.g., "Planet"), and click OK.
  5. Paste the copied signature into the edit box below (using Ctrl+V).
  6. On the right, under "Choose default signature", select your new signature for "New messages" and "Replies/forwards".
  7. Click "OK".

Instructions: Apple Mail (macOS)

  1. Click on "Copy Signature to Clipboard".
  2. Open the Mail app.
  3. In the menu bar, go to "Mail" > "Settings..." (or "Preferences...").
  4. Click the "Signatures" tab.
  5. Select your email account on the left and click the + icon to create a new signature.
  6. Paste the copied signature into the right-hand panel.
  7. IMPORTANT: Uncheck the box labeled "Always match my default message font". Otherwise, the formatting will be destroyed.
  8. Drag the name of your new signature (middle column) onto your email account (left column) to assign it.

Important Note for Mobile Apps (iOS/Android)

Most mobile email apps (especially the Gmail app) do not support HTML signatures. They use separate "Mobile Signatures" which often only allow plain text.

The signature you create here will work reliably in browser and desktop clients, but it will not automatically sync to your mobile app. You must set up your mobile signature (if desired) separately in the app's settings.
